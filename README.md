# Project Plan: Optimizing Shipping Cost in Olist Marketplace
## 1. Context – Business Background

Olist operates in Brazil, a country with a vast territory and complex logistics infrastructure. Due to the long distances between sellers and customers, shipping costs (freight_value) can sometimes account for 30–50% of the product price, particularly for orders shipped across different states.

In the Brazilian e-commerce market, customers are highly sensitive to shipping fees. When the shipping cost appears disproportionately high compared to the product price, customers are more likely to abandon their carts or choose alternative sellers.

Records from the olist_order_items_dataset indicate significant variation between product price and freight cost, suggesting that logistics efficiency may strongly influence purchasing decisions and overall sales performance.

## 2. Need – Business Problem

To improve sales performance and customer satisfaction, the Logistics and Growth teams need to better understand how shipping costs impact purchasing behavior.

Specifically, the business needs to answer the following questions:

What is the tolerable threshold for the ratio between shipping cost and product price before customers are discouraged from completing a purchase?

Which states or cities exhibit unusually high shipping costs that may suppress demand?

Which sellers demonstrate inefficient shipping performance, either through high freight charges or suboptimal logistics practices?

Identifying these issues will allow the company to target operational improvements and reduce barriers to purchase.

## 3. Vision – Analytical Approach

To address these challenges, I will develop a Logistics & Sales Analytics Dashboard that highlights the relationship between shipping costs and sales performance.

The dashboard will include:

1. Price vs Freight Scatter Plot
A visualization showing the relationship between product price and shipping cost. This analysis helps identify products whose sales may be negatively impacted by disproportionately high shipping fees.

2. Geographic Freight Heatmap
A map displaying the average shipping cost by state or region. This will help identify geographic areas where logistics inefficiencies may be driving higher delivery costs.

3. Seller Logistics Scorecard
A ranking system evaluating sellers based on their shipping efficiency, highlighting those with unusually high shipping costs compared to similar products.

These analytical tools will provide actionable insights for both the logistics and seller management teams.

## 4. Outcome – Expected Business Impact

The results of this analysis will support several operational and strategic improvements.

Targeted shipping subsidies
The company may introduce free-shipping or subsidized shipping policies in high-potential regions where high freight costs are currently limiting demand.

Seller logistics optimization
The Seller Management team can work directly with the top sellers with the highest shipping costs, encouraging them to store inventory in fulfillment centers closer to major customer regions.

Measurable performance improvement
The goal of this initiative is to reduce the order abandonment rate due to high shipping costs from 15% to 10% within two quarters, which could potentially increase net revenue by approximately 5%.
